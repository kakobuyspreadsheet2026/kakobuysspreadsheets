Kakobuy spreadsheet shipping: estimated vs actual weight

March 9, 2026 · 4 min read

One of the most common surprises after using a Kakobuy spreadsheet is that international shipping costs can change after packing. That usually happens because the original quote is only an estimate.

Why the first shipping number changes

Before packing, Kakobuy may show an estimated weight based on the item type or seller information. After the warehouse packs the parcel, the final price can change because the actual package weight or volumetric size is different.

  • Heavy items can cost more than the first estimate.
  • Bulky boxes can trigger volumetric pricing.
  • Multi-item hauls often change the most after packing.

When rehearsal shipping helps

Rehearsal is useful when you combine multiple spreadsheet finds into one outbound parcel. It gives you a better preview of packed size before you choose a final line.

This is especially helpful for shoes, coats, and boxed items where volume can matter as much as grams.

How to reduce surprise costs

  • Shortlist smarter in the spreadsheet and avoid unnecessary duplicates.
  • Use rehearsal when the line supports it.
  • Compare speed, price, and restrictions before final checkout.
  • Budget with a cushion instead of trusting the first quote exactly.

Best flow for buyers

Use the spreadsheet hub to compare products, then review the eight-step Kakobuy guides, and only make the final shipping choice after Kakobuy shows the packed result.

Final postage, exchange rate, and shipping line rules are all determined inside Kakobuy, not on this site.
